How does solar energy work?

Solar Panels

A solar panel is used to gather energy. The solar panel will gather energy when the sun is out. If the sun is not out, the solar panel will not gather energy. If the sun is out, however, the solar panel will gather energy. It collects energy by converting the rays of light into energy. This energy is transferred to other components of the solar energy home system to eventually power your home appliances.

Charge Controller

The solar charge controller directs energy to the battery bank. It ensures the battery bank is always full without overflowing. If there is too much energy, the solar charge controller ensures that the battery will not overflow by blocking off excess energy. This way, the solar charge controller will ensure that the energy stored in the battery is always topped off.

Battery system

The battery system stores energy. It stores the energy that your solar panels collect during the day. Usually your solar panels will collect more energy than you need, so a battery will store the excess energy. A battery system can store lots of energy. A battery system is necessary to have, even if you own just a small one, since the sun is not always out to power your solar panels.

DC Power

DC Power or Direct Current Power. It is not the same as AC Power. DC Power means the electricity will flow in one given direction. An inverter inverts the type of power. This way you can save some energy since certain types of power use more power than other powers. By saving this power, you can store more of the energy that your solar panels generated.

Inverter

The inverter converts types of power into another type of power. By converting the types of power, you can control in which direction the flow of energy will move. By controlling the direction in which the flow of energy will move, you can ensure your system does not suffer energy loss due to having to travel longer distances over DC Power.

AC Power

AC Power is a type of power flow. Unlike direct current (DC) Power, AC Power can go both ways. Being able to travel in either direction will spare you from losing any power that your solar panels have generated. Some power will still be lost, but with AC Power, you're less likely to lose power. The power will go in both directions. AC Power. This way, you lose less energy than with DC Power.
